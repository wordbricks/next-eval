What triggers depression?

Depression is often triggered by a combination of genetic, environmental, and psychological factors. Identifying these triggers can help in managing and mitigating its effects. By understanding the root causes, you can develop strategies to improve your mental well-being and overall quality of life.

Restlessness happens when muscles in your forehead, your jaw and the base of your skull tense up. As you start to worry, they tighten up around your head. Everyone’s head muscles tense during stress, but sometimes they tense too much. As the blood flow reduces the more intense the pressure becomes. This increases the discomfort causing the headaches to become stronger and stronger. Despite restlessness being really PAINFUL, you’re most likely completely aware of the sensation you’re feeling.

Regardless of the cause, 30% people feel restless frequently whether they acknowledge it or not.2
